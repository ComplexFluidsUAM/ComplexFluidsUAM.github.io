Making sense of QCM data

In a few years, biosensors will analyse the quality of air and water, detect pathogens and toxins, and provide early diagnoses for cancer patients. To make these technologies possible, we need a correct understanding of the underlying complex nanoscale soft matter and biophysical interactions.By deciphering the underlying molecular dynamics, we enable the design and testing of biosensing devices.

Software for Soft Matter

We apply our knowledge of fluctuating hydrodynamics and mesoscale molecular interactions to reveal the complex motion that takes place at the point of contact between a biosensor and the sample.In addition to theoretical insight, we carry out rigorous numerical simulations with high-performance computing algorithms developed only recently.
